EMPOWERING INDUSTRIES WITH RADIO-ISOTOPES PREDICTION

Predictive methods, particularly classification techniques in machine learning, are essential in industries such as manufacturing, enabling accurate forecasts and informed decision-making. By leveraging historical data and real-time sensor readings, predictive models optimize production processes, minimize downtime, and prevent equipment failures, leading to improved efficiency and cost savings. In our project, we focus on predicting the necessary isotope in businesses, recognizing the significance of accurate prediction for efficient operations and cost-effectiveness. Through a literature review, we explore existing studies on predictive modeling in manufacturing, classification techniques, and data security measures. Our methodology involves the selection of classification techniques for isotope prediction, utilizing relevant data sources for model training, and implementing an encryption standard system to secure the data. Results from our predictive models are presented, showcasing metrics such as accuracy, precision, and recall, along with insights gained from the prediction process. Discussion of the results evaluates the effectiveness of the chosen classification techniques and discusses implications for businesses and industries. Ultimately, our study contributes to the field of predictive modeling and data security in businesses, offering insights for future research in this area.
